Methods

↥
  • All of Oregon's community colleges (17) selected in NCES college navigator and all but LBCC manually traversed to find whether a computer science transfer degree of any form was offered, because NCES program browser was found to be unreliable and their broad categories did not map consistently to Oregon's transfer degrees
  • NCES data on colleges (including transfer rate, net cost, campus setting) was exported into CSV for inclusion in results and purposes of comparative analysis. Program-specific tuition rates would have been very useful, but it seems to be impractical to manually collect it all. It was noted when the program page explicitly and immediately advertised all/many of the CS classes could be taken online.
  • Evaluation (my opinion) of specific institutions was approached based on closest and cheapest options. Acquiring data about quality of specific programs did not appear viable.

Conclusions

↥
  • In person accessibility seems really poor unless a student wants to relocate to be close to another CC. There's several online options in Oregon, but a big caveat is that it's impossible to tell what's available solely online (with PCC as sole exception??) and the information is gated behind reaching out to advisors at each institution. Shouldn't be a super long section.
  • Another paragraph cautiously approaching how hard it is to actually transfer credits to a university of choice could be worthwhile. ASOT-CS, various college-specific AS do not guarantee that everything will transfer - nor do MTMs. Information about this was also gated behind requesting information from advisors, but much more understandably

Recommendations

↥
  • For those who wish to go in-person: Chemeketa and Lane (both also OSU DPP schools) This does not necessarily mean the program is closely aligned.
  • For those who are okay with going online, only PCC (will recheck) seems to explicitly advertise that all 'core CS?' classes can be taken remotely. Will have to be a week recommendation dependent on student checking with a university of choice on how aligned the program is. other schools seem to usually offer at least some courses online (will need to look a bit closer)
